# Social Listening Research Memo

**Stories analyzed:** 1324 public stories and posts from 2026-05-09 to 2026-05-23.

**Attention and tone:** how much attention an issue is receiving and whether coverage is mostly positive, negative, or mixed.

## Biggest shifts in discussion
- **Fastest-rising discussion:** Affordability / cost of living (discussion is about 20% above recent baseline).
- **Most sustained attention:** Affordability / cost of living has the highest story volume.
- **Most negative tone:** Housing / rent has the most concerned coverage language.
- **Strongest attention-and-tone signal:** Corruption / competence / trust combines high volume with stronger concern language.

## Issues with rising attention
- **Corruption / competence / trust**: Test (near recent baseline, attention-and-tone signal 5.6).
- **Affordability / cost of living**: Watch (discussion is about 20% above recent baseline, attention-and-tone signal 3.9).
- **Immigration / public safety**: Watch (discussion is about 15% above recent baseline, attention-and-tone signal 3.9).
- **Housing / rent**: Watch (near recent baseline, attention-and-tone signal 4.1).
- **AI / tech jobs**: Watch (near recent baseline, attention-and-tone signal 4.0).

## Likely concern behind the discussion
- **Affordability / cost of living:** households feeling squeezed by prices, taxes, utilities, and daily expenses.
- **Immigration / public safety:** residents looking for order, fairness, and visible quality-of-life management.
- **Housing / rent:** renters and families worried that stable housing is moving out of reach.
- **AI / tech jobs:** workers asking whether economic change will create opportunity or displacement.
- **Corruption / competence / trust:** voters questioning whether institutions can deliver honestly and competently.

## Recommended message hypotheses
- **Affordability / cost of living:** Connect cost pressure to concrete household relief and fiscal discipline.
- **Affordability / cost of living:** Test whether local examples of lower everyday costs outperform generic affordability language.
- **Immigration / public safety:** Separate compassion, order, and quality-of-life concerns in transparent language.
- **Immigration / public safety:** Test whether operational competence language lowers heat around safety narratives.
- **Housing / rent:** Frame housing as stability, neighborhood continuity, and faster delivery of units.
- **Housing / rent:** Test problem-solving language on permitting, vacancies, and tenant protections.
- **AI / tech jobs:** Emphasize worker readiness, regional opportunity, and guardrails for automation.
- **AI / tech jobs:** Test jobs-and-training language against broader innovation competitiveness framing.
- **Corruption / competence / trust:** Lead with accountability, procurement clarity, and measurable delivery standards.
- **Corruption / competence / trust:** Test a trust-and-results message against a stronger anti-corruption contrast.

## What should be tested next
- Convert watch/test issues into candidate message arms: economic frame, values/trust frame, and competence/problem-solving frame.
- Use public-discourse context features to prioritize research questions and design adaptive experiments.
- Define rewards around engagement quality, surveys, signups, and other consent-based signals without claiming individual persuasion.

## Limitations
- Public data only; no private voter data is used.
- Keyword classification is transparent but approximate.
- Sentiment and attention-and-tone scores are simple research triage signals, not truth labels.
- The experiment log is simulated and intended only to show future adaptive experimentation design.
- Production use would require legal/privacy review, platform compliance, and experimental safeguards.
